Introduction
Sri Lanka Telecom Services (SLTS) is seeking a comprehensive ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) system to streamline operations, improve data accuracy, and enhance decision-making. This proposal outlines a modular ERP solution addressing SLTS’s specific requirements across Inventory Management, SOD Management, Project Management, Office Administration, Subcontractor Management, and Service Assurance Management, with a centralized dashboard for real-time reporting.
Problems
1. Disparate Systems and Data Silos
Multiple Systems: Using separate spreadsheets, legacy software, and manual processes causes difficulties in maintaining data consistency and visibility.
Lack of Real-time Data: Decision-makers may not have immediate access to accurate information, leading to delayed or suboptimal decisions.
2. Inefficient Inventory and SOD Management
Manual Stock Tracking: Relying on manual updates for goods received, returned, and transferred between warehouses/store locations, leading to errors and stock discrepancies.
Unclear Material Usage: Contractor and RTOM-wise material usage may not be tracked properly, causing overspending or material shortages.
3. Complex Project Management
Fragmented Project Tracking: 
There is a lack of an integrated system to handle project estimation, costing, supply chain management, 
and resource allocation.
Issue Management and Accountability: Delays in addressing bottlenecks or issues due to poor visibility of project status and responsibilities.
4. Lack of Transparency in Subcontractor Relations
Registration and Credential Management: Difficulty in maintaining a unified record of subcontractors’, contracts, and performance.
Invoice and Payment Delays: Manual invoicing and payment processes result in late or incorrect payments to subcontractors.
Material Reconciliation: Tracking what materials were used by which subcontractor and reconciling with returned/unused items often becomes cumbersome and error
 
prone.
5. Office Administration Bottlenecks
Vehicle and Rent Management: Multiple contracts, varying rent schedules, and fuel costs are often managed on spreadsheets, leading to missed deadlines or inaccurate budgeting.
Poor Cost Visibility: No consolidated view of recurring office expenses (rent, vehicles, fuel, etc.) to optimize overheads and manage budgets.
6. Ineffective Service Assurance & Workforce Planning
Work Progress Visibility: Difficulty in tracking daily team counts, fieldwork progress, and resource allocation
.
Poor Analytics: Lack of a unified dashboard to monitor service levels, identify gaps, and address them proactively.
7. Limited Reporting & Analytics
Manual Reporting: Creating management reports typically requires collecting data from multiple systems/spreadsheets, leading to manual errors and time-consuming processes.
Lack of Predictive Insights: Without integrated data and analytics, it is challenging to forecast trends, plan for demand changes, or optimize resource allocation.
8. Security and Compliance Concerns
Data Security: Storing critical information in spreadsheets or non-centralized systems can lead to unauthorized access, data corruption, or loss.
Audit Trails & Accountability: Manual processes do not leave clear audit trails, making it difficult to investigate discrepancies or demonstrate compliance.
